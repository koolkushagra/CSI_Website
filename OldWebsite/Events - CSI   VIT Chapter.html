Event Summary

CSI in association with Neo Techonology, organized an introductory session on Graph Databases, Neo4j and the Cipher query language. A graph database is a database that uses graph structures with nodes, edges and properties to represent and store information. Neo4j is a robust (fully ACID) graph database. Due to its graph data model, Neo4j is highly agile and blazing fast. For connected data operations, Neo4j runs a thousand times faster than relational databases. As a technology, Neo4j is being used by big multinational IT and non-IT companies like Cisco, Amazon, Accenture, T-Mobile, Mozilla, Lufthansa, FiftyThree, Adobe and HP. The students were asked to carry their laptops and were provided with high end server of Neo4j.

Event Summary

Switch Coding was a novelty which not only tested the programming skills of the participants but also their ability to code systematically under pressure. In this event, the contestants were required to come in teams of two. Each team was provided with two systems and given five programs to code in a total of 60 minutes of time. Only one member of the team was allowed to code at a time and was given 2 minutes, after which the screen would freeze and the second member would continue writing the program on his screen. The first round had a total of 18 such switches and the teams that were able to complete their programs in the minimum time were selected for the second round. In the second round, each team was given 3 programs to and to raise the bar, the swapping time was reduced to 1 minute. The team that solved their codes in the least time was declared the winner.

Event Summary

Laser Tag was a fun informal event organized for the first time in VIT by an external in which the teamwork, strategy and aiming skills of the participants were tested. In this event, participants registered in teams of five. Each person was provided with a laser tag gun which had a laser sensor, a laser emitter, a life counter, an ammo counter and a shield feature. The sensors were placed on the gun. Every time the sensor was hit by a laser, the life counter was reduced by one. The goal of the event was simple, to aim for the sensors of the other team while protecting the sensors of their own team. When the timer runs out, the team with the maximum number of lives remaining wins. The event took place in the laser tag arena which consisted of a variety of obstacles and objects for taking cover and strategic game play.
